Cybersecurity Note #2

CIA TRIAD

This is one of the most fundamental concepts in cybersecurity.

Almost everything in cybersecurity can be linked back to these three principles.


What is the CIA Triad?

The CIA Triad is a security model made up of:

* Confidentiality
* Integrity
* Availability

It helps organizations protect information and maintain secure systems.



1. Confidentiality

Confidentiality means ensuring that information is only accessible to authorized individuals.

In simple terms:

The right people can see the information.
The wrong people cannot.

Examples

* Passwords
* Bank account details
* Medical records
* Personal messages

How Confidentiality is Protected

* Strong passwords
* Encryption
* Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
* Access controls


2. Integrity

Integrity means ensuring that information remains accurate, complete, and unaltered.

In simple terms:

Data should not be changed without permission.

Example

Imagine a student scores 85 in an exam.

If someone changes the score to 45 without authorization, integrity has been compromised.

How Integrity is Protected

* Hashing
* Digital signatures
* File permissions
* Audit logs


3. Availability

Availability means ensuring that systems and information are accessible when needed.

In simple terms:

Authorized users should be able to access resources whenever they need them.

Example

An online banking system should be available when customers want to check their accounts.

If the system crashes or becomes unavailable, availability is affected.

How Availability is Protected

* Backups
* Redundant systems
* Disaster recovery plans
* System maintenance


Real-Life Example

Consider an online banking application:

Confidentiality

Only the account owner can view their account information.

Integrity

Account balances cannot be altered without authorization.

Availability

Customers should be able to access the banking platform whenever they need it.


Key Takeaway

The CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity.

A secure system should:

* Protect information from unauthorized access (Confidentiality)
* Keep information accurate and trustworthy (Integrity)
* Ensure information remains accessible (Availability)

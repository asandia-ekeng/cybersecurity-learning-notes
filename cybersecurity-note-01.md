Cybersecurity Note 01

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, applications, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, damage, or theft.

In simple terms, cybersecurity is about keeping digital information safe.

Why Cybersecurity Matters

As technology becomes part of everyday life, cyber threats continue to increase.

Cybersecurity helps protect:

* Personal information
* Business data
* Financial systems
* Government infrastructure
* Online services

Common Cybersecurity Threats

Phishing

Fraudulent messages designed to trick people into revealing sensitive information.

Example:
A fake email pretending to be from a bank requesting login credentials.

Malware

Malicious software designed to damage systems or steal information.

Examples include:

* Viruses
* Worms
* Ransomware

Weak Passwords

Simple passwords can be easily guessed or cracked by attackers.

Example:

* 123456
* password

Best Security Practices

* Use strong passwords
* Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
* Keep software updated
* Verify links before clicking
* Back up important data regularly

Key Takeaway

Cybersecurity is not only for security professionals.

Anyone who uses a smartphone, computer, or the internet benefits from basic cybersecurity knowledge.

Learning cybersecurity starts with understanding common threats and adopting safe online habits.

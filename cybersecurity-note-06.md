Cybersecurity Learning Note #6

Social Engineering (Human Hacking)

🧠 What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is a type of cyber attack where attackers manipulate people instead of systems to gain access to information, systems, or money.

Instead of hacking computers…

They hack humans.


🎭 Simple Example

An attacker does NOT break into a system.

Instead they might:

* Pretend to be IT support
* Send a fake email
* Call pretending to be a bank agent
* Trick someone into revealing a password


⚠️ Why it works

Social engineering works because it exploits human behavior:

* Trust
* Fear
* Urgency
* Curiosity
* Authority


🧪 Common Social Engineering Attacks

1. Phishing

Fake emails or messages that trick users into clicking links or entering passwords.


2. Pretexting

Attacker creates a fake scenario to get information.

Example:

“I’m from HR, confirm your login details.”


3. Baiting

Offering something tempting (free downloads, gifts) to infect a device.


4. Impersonation

Pretending to be someone trustworthy (boss, bank, IT staff).


5. Tailgating (Physical)

Following someone into a secure building without permission.


🚨 Real-World Insight

Most major data breaches do NOT start with coding errors.

They start with:

One human mistake.

One click.

One password shared.


🛡️ How to Protect Yourself

* Always verify requests
* Never share passwords
* Be suspicious of urgency
* Check email addresses carefully
* Use MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)
* Think before clicking links


🧩 Key Takeaway

The weakest part of any system is often the human using it.


💡 Reflection

Cybersecurity is not just about technology.

It is about understanding:

how humans think, behave, and get tricked.
